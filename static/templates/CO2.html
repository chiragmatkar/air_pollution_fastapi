Carbon Dioxide

Carbon dioxide (CO2) classifies as a dominant air pollutant. Moderately high concentrations of CO2 in indoor air can lead to drowsiness, fatigue, and headaches, and increasing amounts can cause dizziness and nausea [1]. At extremely high concentrations, CO2 can even cause displacement asphyxia [2]. In our experimental data, we also see increases in CO2 during indoor exercise events. The simple process of improving ventilation can reduce high concentrations of CO2 in homes and workplaces and improve productivity and comfort.

Health effects of varying levels of CO2 are as follows: 351-450ppm is a healthy outdoor level, 451-700ppm is normal, 701-1000ppm is acceptable, 1001-2500ppm causes drowsiness and other symptoms of discomfort, 2501-5000ppm is detrimental to health, and anything higher than 5000ppm is extremely unhealthy [3].
